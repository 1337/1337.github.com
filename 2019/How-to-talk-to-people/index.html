How to talk to people

炸 [zhá]

I make my living talking to people. Nobel Prize winners, truck drivers, billionaires, kindergarten teachers, heads of state, plumbers… I talk to people that I like; I talk to people that I don’t like; I talked to some people that I disagree with deeply on a personal level, but I still have a great conversation with them.

Don’t multitask.

Be in that moment. Don’t think about anything else. Remove your conversational filters and talk, and share (aka “give and take”). If you think about your filters, you stop paying attention.

There is no reason to learn how to show you’re paying attention if you are in fact paying attention.

After “that moment”

  • Think about what happened and what was discussed.
  • Assess your body language that you used at the time.
  • Assess your body language that the other people used at the time.

Don’t pontificate. (3.1)

v. To express opinions or judgments in a dogmatic way.

Set your opinions aside. Enter every conversation assuming you have something to learn.

This also means: be a listener.

Use open-ended questions.

Don’t ask questions that can be answered with “yes” or “no”. If you don’t know how to do that, ask questions that start with “who”, “what”, “when”, “where”, or “why”, all of which guarantee an answer that is not “yes” or “no”.

Go with the flow.

Conversation ideas can come and go. You might think of a clever thing to ask two minutes before the person stopped talking, at which point it is no longer appropriate to ask it. Let them come and let them go.

If you don’t know, say that you don’t know.

Don’t claim to be an expert on anything.

Don’t equate your experience with theirs. (3.2)

It is never the same. All experiences are individual. Don’t take the moment to share your experience.

But remember don’t be afraid to be vulnerable. Everyone is flawed in some way. Don’t be afraid to show it.

Try not to repeat yourself.

It’s condescending.

Stay out of the weeds.

No one cares about the details (weeds). Leave them out.

LISTEN. (2.4)

If your mouth is open, you’re not learning. - Claims to be said by a buddha
He who talks more is sooner exhausted. - Lao Tzu

People love to talk about the things that matter to them the most. (2.4) Listen to them. Talk to them about those things. (2.5)
You have to listen with the intent to understand, not just the intent to answer.

Be brief.

Be genuinely interested in other people. Everyone has some hidden, amazing things about them. Keep your mouth shut for as long as possible. (2.1)

Smile. (2.2)

Fake it ‘til you make it.

Call people by their names. (2.3)

People love their own names.

If all else fails

Stick to the weather (excluding “global warming is a hoax”) and your health (excluding topics about vaccines).
