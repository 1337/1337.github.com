Quick Tuna Sandwich

Believe it or not, the average person on the Internet can write a useless post about making a cheese tuna sandwich using a sandwich maker (which may go by the term ‘pie iron’). I must emphasise that we are not making a grilled cheese today, since some people can be very pedantic about these things.

Ingredients

  • 50g light flaked tuna
  • 20g faux cheese
  • 2 slices bread
  • Mayonnaise (optional)
  • Butter (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat sandwich maker for 3 minutes.
  2. Apply mayonnaise to one side of both slices of bread, or, should butter be used, one slice per condiment.
  3. Place one slice of bread, mayonnaise side up, in the sandwich maker.
  4. Spread cheese, then tuna, evenly onto the bread.
  5. Place remaining slice of bread, mayonnaise side down, onto the structure.
  6. Close sandwich maker. Hold for 5 minutes.
  7. Remove sandwich and serve.

Vegetarian version

To adapt this recipe for vegetarians, simply remove the tuna and replace it with nothing.

Quick Chili

Chili is the vegetarian staple, obviously. Unfortuntely, the “Carne” in Chili con carne literally means meat (in Spanish? I think), so it does take meat to make a legitimate chili.

Ingredients

  • 500g diced tomatoes
  • 250g any mixture of beans
  • 100g ground beef (optional)
  • 100g chopped onions (optional)
  • 25g chili
  • 5g spices
  • 5g salt (optional)

Instructions

  1. If ground beef is used, brown it in a pan.
  2. Mix all ingredients in a pot.
  3. Boil in low heat for two hours, or until the mixture is no longer watery.

I Have Developed the Formula for University Stinky Tofu

The steps for producing a smelly piece of tofu is as follows:

  1. Add to a hot pan, a piece of tofu. Fry until golden.
  2. Reduce heat to medium and wait for water to dry up.
  3. Add to pan a portion of peanut butter. Roll tofu on top of it.
  4. Raise heat to max.
  5. Smelly tofu will be done when all peanut butter is melted and burnt.

Not only does it stink like heck, it also smells like real smelly tofu! Enjoy!
