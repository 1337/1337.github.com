Citation Needed

Greg Wilson, former professor, wants us to have critical thinking skills in the field of software development. We/you cannot just 1) blindly accept what people say about best practices, 2) do low-quality experiments, especially if your degree was computer science, rather than computer engineering.

  1. You will produce roughly the same number of lines per hour, regardless of language.
  2. If you believe a task is based on aptitude/talent, you will do worse.
  3. For every 25% increase in problem complexity, there is a 100% increase in solution complexity. This means you can drop 25% problem complexity in exchange for being twice as fast.
  4. If you need to rewrite more than 25% of a component (avionics in particular), it is better to rewrite it from scratch.
  5. Having someone read your code for an hour before tests are written is more time-efficient than having tests pass. But do not pass the one hour mark however, the brain fills up after one hour.
  6. Physical distance between developers has no impact on post-release fault rate. What does, is how far apart people are in the org chart.
  7. Far fewer people actually use UML diagrams to think than what textbooks make you think.

  8. The next time someone says “Haskell is good” I want you to ask, “citation please, not your anecdote, have you tested this?”

  9. University doesn’t get us to learn; university gets us to ask good questions. University teaches you how to take over the world, whether or not you want to. The time will come, very quickly, when you must run the world.
