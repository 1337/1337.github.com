Easy Autism Test

Content warning: this article discusses nothing in particular.

Self diagnosis of autism is not reliable; we should not be diagnosing ourselves with anything. Self-diagnosis has high false negative and false positive rates, properties not desirable in an experiment.

You should go to a psychologist for such diagnoses. All, though, people are increasingly diagnosed with autism—which is no surprise, considering almost everyone has a disorder of some kind. To diagnose yourself with autism, without the disadvantages of self-diagnosis, single blind tests should be conducted, using participants who do not know that the test is being conducted, and who/m know you relatively well.

Two of the three following methods require a non-reproducible setting, where you are already holding a conversation with your participants. The procedures cannot be reproduced, on par with other modern psychology experimental standards.

Method 1: De Facto Association

Procedure

Hold a conversation with your participant. Without revealing your intention, state that

Since as you know I am autistic, I [do something completely normal, such as drawing].

Results

If the participant thinks that you are autistic, they will not have reacted to such a statement.
On the other hand, should the participant find you a healthy, normal human being, you will be asked why you said that.

Method 2: Proof by Negation

The second method, derived from the million-dollar social experiment, uses two preposterous premises in order to reveal the one that is more ridiculous, depending on setting.

Procedure

Hold a conversation with your participant. Without revealing your intention, state that

Since I am autistic and [the opposite of your sexual orientation], I [do something completely normal, such as drawing].

Results

If the participant thinks that you are autistic, their response will not contain the autism component, such as “I didn’t know you are [straight]’”.
On the other hand, should the participant find you a healthy, normal human being, you will be responded with “What?”.

Method 3: Scientific Method

The third, and perhaps the easiest method, but with by far the highest false negative rates, is by directly stating your hypothesis to your participant:

[Participant’s name], I have autism.

If you do that, you have autism.

Alternatively, just write an article like this one. Then, when you finish writing, you will know for sure that you have autism.
