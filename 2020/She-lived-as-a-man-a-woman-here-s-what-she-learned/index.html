She lived as a man & a woman -- here's what she learned

Paula Stone Williams was a man and now a transgender woman. By living life on both sides, she gets to see the world… at least from more perspectives than most of us. Other trans people speak out, too. Let’s see what they have to say, before and after changing gender.

Life as a man, pros

  • People respect you.
  • People take (you, and what you talk about) seriously. This eliminates a man’s need to doubt himself.
  • People don’t assume you are stupid.
  • Men typically aren’t mean to other men.
  • Men don’t need to apologise for being right.
  • Men don’t have periods. They also never hurt due to pregnancy and childbirth.
  • People rarely spike your drinks. Although male rape does exist, it is reported much less frequently.
  • Women don’t (sexually) harass you or, to a lesser extent, your appearance.
  • It is not scary to walk alone at night.
  • Typically, no makeup is required to appear normal.
  • A man’s attractiveness does not drop off sharply at age 30.
  • It is much easier for you to pursue a leadership role if you are a man.
  • The vast majority of organised religions don’t oppress men.

The little things:

  • Jeans pockets can actually hold things.
  • Things never fall out of any pocket.
  • Male skin is thicker and more protective.
  • Clothing sizing numbers actually mean something.

Life as a woman, pros

  • It is okay to be short (being fat is another thing, but at least you can control it).
  • Haircuts are required less often.
  • Women live longer.
  • Women can choose to be fabulous.
  • When it comes to dating, you get plenty of choices.
  • Women are far less lonely in this world, and share their feelings and affection.
  • A woman’s value is not derived from her ability to provide.
  • A woman’s occupation is cleaner on average, mainly skewed by some men working sewage, mining, construction, and other forms of hard labour.
  • Less is expected of women: the world is not on their shoulders. Women do not need (at least seen by society) to do great things, and to achieve great heights in their careers.
  • It is socially acceptable to shop as much as you want and not care about your spending habits.
  • Women are free to be emotional—even weak—if so desired.
  • Women can have children, if that’s what you want.
  • Getting lighter sentences for crimes like rape is among the social, political, and legal double standards that prefer women, such as custody.
  • In most countries, women are never conscripted for war.

/u/Zenla8282 put it nicely: I think both men and women have it harder and easier in different ways, but usually when life gets tough, most of us don’t think “wow my life is so hard because I’m a woman/man”.
