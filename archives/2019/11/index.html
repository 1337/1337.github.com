How much energy can you steal from work?

So you are going through some trouble in life, and you are unable to pay your electricity bill. It happens sometimes. But it so happens that you are still employed, and you have plenty of tools to spare. How can you make your company pay for your electricity? How can you make your company work for you?

(All calculations assume electricity costs \$0.10 per kWh.)

Attempt 1: charge your phone

Your phone probably has a 3000mAh battery, more or less. That converts to 11.1Wh of energy at 3.7V. At \$0.10 per kWh, you can get \$0.00111 per day, or about \$0.02 per month if you work 20 days every month.

Attempt 2: use a power bank

You can bring a power bank to work, charge it there, and then bring it back home. Suppose you have one of those big bois that can theoretically store 76Wh in a single charge. Assuming perfect energy transfer, which is never true, you just got yourself 0.076 kWh * \$0.10 kWh⁻¹ = \$0.0076 worth of energy per day, or about \$0.15 per month if you work 20 days every month.

Attempt 3: boil water

Heat can be used to generate electricity if you are smart enough, so if you boil water at work, you can store that heat in a thermos and bring it home to use it there (somehow).

Let’s say you can boil 4 litres of water at work every day without looking suspicious. 4L of water can store 4.1814 J per gK, so if you have 4kg of water and intend to reduce your water from 95°C to 25°C, the energy you can steal from work is 4181 * 4 * 70 J = 1 MJ, or 0.3 kWh. That is actually more energy than charging a battery bank (worth \$0.60 per month). Nice!

Attempt 4: charge your electric car at work

If you are so poor you can’t pay your hydro bill but have a Tesla model X, congratulations! You can steal electricity from work by charging it there.

Your 75 kWh battery (assuming you literally use all of its capacity at home and somehow manage to drive to work after that) earns you \$7.50 every day, or \$150 per month if you work 20 days every month.

Verdict: if you’re too poor to afford electricity, buy a Tesla. Problem solved.

*Well, if you had a 4-kg battery pack, this would not be true.
